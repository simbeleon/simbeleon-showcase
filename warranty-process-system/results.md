# Results & Metrics · Warranty Process System

Concrete outcomes from six years of building and managing the warranty and contract system at HDW Belux.

---

## The Core Number

**Warranty acceptance rate: 63% → 88%**

That's a 25 percentage point improvement. At claim volumes of ±1,660 and average values across the portfolio, that difference translates directly into recovered revenue that would otherwise have been written off.

### What Drove It

The improvement wasn't a single fix — it was a set of structural changes applied consistently over time:

1. **Better intake**: claims submitted with incomplete information got rejected or delayed by manufacturers. The fix was standardized intake forms that captured required data before anything was submitted externally. Fewer incomplete claims, higher first-pass acceptance.

2. **Faster submission**: manufacturers have time windows. Claims submitted late get rejected regardless of validity. Building a tracking system with deadline alerts meant fewer claims fell through the cracks on timing alone.

3. **Documentation discipline**: a rejected claim can often be resubmitted with additional evidence. Without a log of what was submitted and what was requested, follow-up was inconsistent. Systematic documentation made resubmission tractable.

4. **Pattern recognition**: after processing hundreds of claims, patterns in manufacturer rejection reasons became clear. That knowledge was built back into the intake process — pre-empting the most common rejection grounds before submission.

## Maintenance Contracts

±150 active maintenance contracts across major utility operators in Belgium required ongoing management:

- Contract terms reviewed and mapped to service eligibility
- Renewal cycles tracked to prevent lapses
- SLA performance monitored per contract
- Discrepancies between contract scope and actual service delivery flagged and resolved

The utility client base (Ores, Sibelga, Equans, Fluvius) operates under strict compliance requirements. Contract documentation needed to be accurate, current, and audit-ready at all times.

## What the Numbers Don't Show

The acceptance rate and claim volumes are measurable. What's harder to quantify:

- **Institutional knowledge captured**: the process documentation means the system doesn't depend on one person's memory
- **Manufacturer relationships**: consistent, well-documented communication builds credibility with manufacturer warranty departments over time
- **Internal trust**: reliable reporting means management decisions are made on accurate data, not estimates
